## Learnings

### 2026-07-21 — Customer-owned governance settings register

- Governance tracking must become a customer artifact from day 1 (not a training scaffolding removed at the end). Linking every register row to customer-owned locations (repos, policy docs) ensures the register survives the engagement and drives ongoing audit/review.
- Balance prescriptive and flexible: template provides structure (domain/setting/level/evidence fields) but lets customers fill in rationale and implementation timelines based on their org's risk posture.
- `approved pilot` vs `inspect-and-propose` distinction allows honest assessment: some settings (e.g., base permission `read`) are ready to deploy; others (e.g., 2FA enforcement) need org-owner risk approval first. Training shouldn't hide that distinction.
- Cumulative design prevents fragmentation: one register across all governance activities, not one doc per challenge. Coaches verify one place; customers audit one place.
- Terminology sensitivity matters: using "activity" instead of "challenge" in reader-facing text reinforces the customer delivery mindset (ownership, not instruction).

### 2026-07-21 — Governance domain coverage

- 11 GHEC activities now feed the register: development (devcontainers/prebuilds), workflow (Actions policy/cache), org-membership (permissions/creation), teams (hierarchy/roles), repo-policy (rulesets/properties), audit (logs/streaming), identity (SSO/SAML), security (scanning/secrets/code-quality), and operations (runners). Coverage is broad but not exhaustive; missing: approval/code-owner rules, branch protection beyond rulesets, SAML-driven team sync (enterprise-only), and cost optimization dashboards.
- Field `effective level` (enterprise/org/repo) helps learners reason about scope and inheritance. E.g., org-level setting affects all repos unless overridden by a stricter repo ruleset.
- No duplication: Ch06 governs org-baseline; Ch04/07/08 govern downstream (teams/rulesets). Ch04 doesn't re-teach org-membership; it focuses on workflow guardrails and registers its own rows.

### 2026-07-21 — Evidence collection strategy

- Success criteria now explicitly ask for register rows with evidence links, not just task completion. This shifts mindset from "I checked the box" to "I can prove it."
- Evidence types vary: API snapshots (jq output), decision docs (Markdown), workflow runs (GitHub URL), config exports (YAML), and screenshots. Coaches should spot-check that links are real, not placeholders.
- Customer delivery teams will argue about retention timelines (e.g., "How long do we keep the API snapshots?"). Register includes a "retention/review cadence" column for that conversation.

### 2026-07-21 — Governance register as cumulative artifact

- Unlike one-off activity outcomes (a workflow, a ruleset, a token), the register is cumulative: each activity adds to it. By the end of the engagement, the register is the master inventory of all governance decisions made.
- Learners should pass the register forward at handover: org owner inherits not just approved settings but also the decision rationale, evidence links, and review schedule.
- Future: Auto-generate a PDF or audit trail from the register for compliance reports.
