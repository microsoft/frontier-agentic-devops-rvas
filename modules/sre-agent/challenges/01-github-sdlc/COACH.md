# Coach Guide: Challenge 01 — Establish the GitHub SDLC

### Expected Outcome

A rough request becomes a clear issue, branch, pull request, review, and visible quality signal. The issue is also suitable as a first agent-ready specification.

## Grounding conversation (you will be called)

Students are **expected to call you** to talk through this challenge's real-world impact before they consider it done. This is a required completion step, not optional — it is how we keep the learning grounded in their actual day-to-day work.

**Their question:** Coach conversation — if you applied this GitHub SDLC baseline to the next real change request your team receives, which step (issue clarity, branch linking, review evidence, or quality gate) would be most likely to slip, and why? Talk it through with your coach and connect it to a real project, task, or workflow you own.

Use these follow-ups to steer the conversation:
- Ask them to name a specific service, repo, or team where this would be the next real change request.
- Ask what evidence their current process would be missing if the change went wrong — who would be asked first, and why that person instead of a commit or issue?
- Ask them to commit to one concrete improvement (e.g., an issue template, a required review check, or a branch naming rule) they will put in place before their next production change.

### Strong Evidence

- Issue includes user impact, acceptance criteria, and validation.
- Issue includes boundaries, relevant files, non-goals, validation commands, and human checkpoints.
- Pull request references the issue.
- Review comments discuss risk or correctness.
- A check, test, or manual validation result is visible.

### Common Gaps

- Issue is only a title.
- Pull request includes unrelated changes.
- Review is reduced to a rubber-stamp approval.
- Validation is described verbally but not recorded.
- Agent-ready details are assumed from team conversation rather than written.

### Coach Hint

Ask: If a teammate joined tomorrow, could they reconstruct why this change happened and why it was safe?
